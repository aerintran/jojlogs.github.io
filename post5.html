march 27, 2023

Exponentially advancing technology

Currently our foes include iPad kids, Elon Musk, and evil AI robots trying to claw their way out of the uncanny valley. Now, most futuristic movies are kind of sad, like Blade Runner, they're usually warnings of rampant technology and the downfall of humanity; which I do think will eventually come to happen (I'm like 78% sure). It will be a time of constant war and conflict, huge fancy laser guns or like mecha soldiers, maybe. Since a robot will be able to perform many human-esque functions, the humanity in robots will come into question parallel with the animalism in humans as the "superior-species" comes into question. Think: robots that are better able to recognize a child in a war-torn country as opposed to trigger-happy soldiers, or like Paul doing the gom jabbar test #italreadyhappenedinDune. So basically the future will be bad for a certain period of time.

Esentially Human

As humans become closer to robots (bc of capitalism) and robots become more like humans, there will be a decline of highly emotional, human-esque elements aka frivolity (like art and stuff) as seen in architectural modernism and sans serif fonts. This would be much more efficient I think, but is generally seen as bad for some reason, but these essentially human emotions are so highly regarded allowing the power of love to save the world, (like Harry Potter. Or My Little Pony), a common and boring cop-out used in films. Well if losing these human emotions is so bad, do we stop or somehow hinder the advancement of technology since it is so evil? No. Because technology improves our lives in many ways. Like how Tiktok knows which Vinnie Hacker videos to put into my feed (I like the car ones). So anyways,how do we prevent technology from destroying our lives? Humans will need to ground themselves in a constantly volatile world, they will need to establish themselves within the matrix. And it will be through religion. Yes, the true religion. Or call it spirituality, if the word religion has been sullied by money laundering Catholic churches for you.

The True Religion

Anyways, they will base these societies on a religion with a foundation of very strong divine and metaphysical connection to their surroundings. People will no longer be able to manipulate nuclear energy for their evil side-quests because people won't understand it as technology, something tamperable by sticky human hands. No. It is magic, a manifestation of spiritual devotion to the greater being, be it a singular figure or the collective peoples themselves. This magic is left unknown and essentially greater than any human. Godly, untouchable. Yes, this great spirituality will stop humans from preying on one another, and it is very much like the Avatar.

Avatar

Tsaheylu (the hair thing) is essentially a neural connection. Very plausible if you consider it is a very advanced neural connection that could allow two brains to communicate through synapses and rudimentary neural impulses without room for misinterpretation through language and culture, it is pure intent not obscured by human formalities or notions. The Tree of Souls is just like a national centralized database. (I could have sworn I read somewhere that the US was gonna make this big black box physically capable of housing an entire nation's worth of data. But I can't find the article now. Strange). Anyways, these very mythical things, such as a connection to surroundings and respecting nature, concepts completely foreign to the modern-day human seem like magic. But what is magic if not extremely advanced science? Show a Victorian-era child an ice-cold bottle of Mountain Dew. How does it literally glow neon green? It must be magic. Therefore sorcery could be backed with some very very much advanced, yet plausible, science. However, don't the Avatar people seem a bit primitive?

Spiral

It will be a reversion, cyclical like all things, like the spiral, get it SPIRituAL. So we will abandon the guns and war ships for spears and arrows, we will sacrifice mass-production for hand-made artisan goods. But we could never completely abandon modern technology for a truly acoustic life. Therefore a select few will leave a new generation in a secluded area (imagine Maze Runner) raised faithful to this true religion in which they will live a life without the poison of evil tech and greed. But aren't we banking too much on human faith? Yes, over time people lose faith. Forget the old religion. And be left to the violent world of greed and materialistic pursuits. And the cycle will restart all over again.
